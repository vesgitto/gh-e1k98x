Steve Cohen, the billionaire hedge fund manager, completed his purchase of the Mets in October 2020, and since then, he has made significant waves in both the organization and the fanbase. In this blog post, we'll dive deep into Cohen's plans for the Mets moving forward and explore how he intends to reshape the franchise that has struggled for decades.

Steve Cohen: A Brief Overview

Before we get into the specifics of Cohen's plans, it’s important to understand the man behind the initiative. Steve Cohen was born in 1956 in Great Neck, New York, and he has been a lifelong fan of the Mets. He made his fortune through Point72 Asset Management, a hedge fund he founded, and has a net worth estimated to be over $15 billion. Unlike previous ownership groups, Cohen's wealth affords him the leeway to invest in the team in a way that can truly transform it into a contender.

Cohen's approach has always been characterized by a willingness to spend. He announced that he intended to create a winning culture, focusing not just on immediate success, but also on building a sustainable foundation for the future. Famous for saying, "I want to be able to say that I built something," Cohen has deduced that his legacy will be tied to the success of the Mets.
